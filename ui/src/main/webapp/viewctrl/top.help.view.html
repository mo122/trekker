About: Treks

Tasks follow treks. Treks are made up of steps. The steps have logical ordering so that we can quickly reason about trek progress.

For maximum actionability all tasks follow exactly one trek. Tasks may jump treks where it is convenient to co-opt them, e.g. a question reveals a subtle bug and changes from the question trek to the bug trek. A task not classifiable under a single trek may indicate a frivolous or immature task that should either be rewritten or removed (closed).

About: Flags

Tasks can be flagged. Flags impart upon a task some notion which supersedes the forefront thinking about the trek itself. "Something about this task is more important than the kind of task that it is (its trek)."

Only a single flag may be present on a task. Additional flags may be informative but become noisy with respect to task tracking. Trekker seeks to maximize the actionability or liquidity of tasks, not to maintain meticulous task tracking metadata. Data encoded into stringent flags often appeases some quasi-OCD tendency and contributes little to the primary goal of Trekker.

About: Themes

A pile of related tasks may be bundled under a Theme.

Themes can be used to reduce intimidating task counts, to capture multiple parts of a larger effort in dividual tasks, and can encourage multiple team members to work together towards a common goal. If a milestone bundles tasks within a time boundary, then a theme bundles goals within a semantic boundary, such as a complex feature, or a concerted effort to pay off a bundle of debts.
